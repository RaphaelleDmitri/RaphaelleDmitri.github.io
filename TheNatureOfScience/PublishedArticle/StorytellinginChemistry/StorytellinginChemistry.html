Storytelling in Chemistry Education

Article Title: Chemistry Takes Center Stage: Stories and Narratives as Tools for Instruction

This article explores the innovative use of storytelling and narratives in chemistry instruction. Rather than relying solely on technical explanations, educators are using stories to connect abstract scientific concepts to real-life contexts.

Highlights:

  • Bridging Conceptual Gaps: Stories help students understand how macroscopic (visible), molecular, and atomistic levels of matter are connected.
  • Engagement Through Narratives: Lessons framed as narratives or case studies foster deeper interest and retention.
  • Instructional Innovation: Using storytelling in science classrooms supports more meaningful learning experiences, especially in subjects like chemistry that students often find challenging.
