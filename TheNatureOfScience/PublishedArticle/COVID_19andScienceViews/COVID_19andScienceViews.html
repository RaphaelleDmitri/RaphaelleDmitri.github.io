Study Title: Views on the Nature of Science, Beliefs, Trust in the Government, and COVID-19 Pandemic Preventive Behavior Among Undergraduate Students

This research examined how Filipino undergraduate students' understanding of NOS, their beliefs about the COVID-19 pandemic, and their trust in government impacted their preventive health behaviors during the pandemic.

student studying science at home during pandemic

Key Findings:

  • Understanding NOS Matters: Students with a stronger grasp of scientific concepts and the nature of science were more likely to follow recommended health practices.
  • Beliefs Influence Behavior: Perceptions about the seriousness and legitimacy of the pandemic shaped personal actions.
  • Government Trust Is Not Enough: Surprisingly, trust in the government alone did not predict behavior, highlighting the importance of independent scientific understanding.

Educational Impact:

The study suggests that enhancing NOS education can empower individuals to make informed decisions during public health crises.
