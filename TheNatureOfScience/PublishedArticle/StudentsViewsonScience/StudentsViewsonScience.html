Study Title: Junior High School Students’ Views of Nature of Science: Evidence from a Private School in Davao Region, Philippines

This study explored how junior high school students in the Philippines perceive the Nature of Science (NOS)—a concept that includes how scientific knowledge is developed, validated, and understood. The findings revealed that students generally hold transitional views, meaning they are in-between naive and informed understandings of NOS concepts.

students learning science

Key Highlights:

  • Imagination and Creativity: Gender differences were observed—suggesting that male and female students may perceive or engage with creative aspects of science differently.
  • Tentativeness and Observation-Inference: Understanding of these aspects varied across grade levels, indicating that exposure and maturity influence comprehension.
  • Educational Implication: There is a clear need for targeted interventions to help students develop a deeper, more accurate view of scientific processes—crucial for nurturing scientific literacy in future generations.
