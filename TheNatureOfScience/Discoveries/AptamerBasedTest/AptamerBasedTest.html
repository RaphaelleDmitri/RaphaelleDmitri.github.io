Aptamer-Based Test for Trichomoniasis Detection

Click here for more details

Researchers from the University of the Philippines Diliman's College of Science have developed a novel diagnostic method for trichomoniasis, a prevalent sexually transmitted infection. Utilizing aptamers—short DNA strands that bind to specific targets—they created a sandwich enzyme-linked aptamer-based assay (ELAA) capable of detecting low concentrations of Trichomonas vaginalis, the parasite responsible for the infection. This advancement promises more affordable and rapid testing options, addressing the urgent need for accurate and accessible diagnostics.
