Scientific Theories and Laws

Scientific theories and laws are fundamental components of the scientific method. Theories explain why phenomena occur, while laws describe how they occur under specific conditions.

Scientific Theories and Laws: What’s the Difference?

A common misconception is that scientific theories become laws with enough evidence. In reality, theories and laws serve different purposes in science, and both are equally important.

Scientific Theories

  • A scientific theory is a well-substantiated explanation of some aspect of the natural world.
  • It is based on a body of evidence and has withstood repeated testing.
  • Examples: Theory of Evolution, Germ Theory of Disease, Theory of Plate Tectonics
  • Theories explain how and why things happen.

Scientific Laws

  • A scientific law is a description of an observed phenomenon, often expressed mathematically.
  • It describes patterns in nature that consistently occur under certain conditions.
  • Examples: Newton’s Laws of Motion, Law of Conservation of Mass, Kepler’s Laws of Planetary Motion
  • Laws describe what happens, but do not necessarily explain why.
