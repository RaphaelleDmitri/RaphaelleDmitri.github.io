Tentativeness of Scientific Knowledge

One of the most important—and often misunderstood—features of science is that scientific knowledge is tentative. This means it is always open to revision as new evidence becomes available.

changing scientific understanding illustration

Why Science Changes

  • New Discoveries: Advancements in technology can reveal information that wasn't previously observable.
  • Reevaluation of Evidence: Existing data may be reinterpreted in light of new theories or perspectives.
  • Peer Review and Replication: Continuous scrutiny by the scientific community helps refine and improve understanding.

scientists reviewing old data

Not a Weakness, But a Strength

The ability to adapt and improve makes science a powerful and self-correcting process. Changing our understanding of the world in response to new information is what drives scientific progress.
